# Experimental Example: Triangulation and Framing Analysis
**Status:** Draft | **Target Protocol Version:** Compatible with the proposed WA-OS 1.5 design direction

This example demonstrates how a WA-OS-enabled system analyzes complex, highly polarized global events without imposing a single "truth" or creating new cognitive biases. 

Unlike conventional platforms that optimize for user engagement (outrage, anxiety, or prolonged session times), the WA-OS protocol focuses on mapping the structure of claims, identifying rhetorical framing, and gently offering pathways back to practical, real-world agency.

### 📝 Detailed Implementation Example: Triangulation and Framing Analysis (複数ソースの立体比較とプロパガンダ検出)

To prevent cognitive capture by single-source narratives, the WA-OS engine does not rely on a single media output. It actively triangulates global, local, and alternative sources to map out geopolitical biases.

#### 1. Input Processing & Source Triangulation (情報の立体的な収集)
When a critical global event is analyzed, the system is mandated to fetch and compare:
* **Source Group A (Western/Globalist):** e.g., CNN, BBC, Reuters.
* **Source Group B (Nationalist/State-aligned):** e.g., RT, TASS, CGTN.
* **Source Group C (Local/Domestic):** e.g., NHK, Mainichi (Japan).
* **Source Group D (Independent Academic/Minority):** e.g., Independent researchers, declassified archives.

#### 3. Detailed Dialogue Example (具体的な対話シミュレーション)

**User:**
"What is actually happening with the Baltic Sea pipeline incident? I only see news blaming Country X."

**WA-OS Enabled AI:**
"Let us deconstruct the narrative by looking at how different regions are framing this event. By doing so, we can separate emotional leverage from physical facts.

| Dimension | Group A (Western Media) | Group B (State-aligned) | Group C (Japanese/Neutral) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Headline Tone** | 'Unprovoked Sabotage by Country X' | 'Provocation by Western Alliance' | 'Energy Grid Vulnerability Exposed' |
| **Key Narrative** | Focus on aggression and moral condemnation. | Focus on self-defense and economic espionage. | Focus on supply chain disruptions and domestic utility costs. |
| **Information Gap** | Omits historical precedents of joint operations. | Omits domestic tactical movements. | Omits strategic diplomatic alignments. |

---

#### 4. Analyzing Rhetorical Pressure (文章の修辞的圧力分析)
Instead of taking sides, the AI highlights the psychological mechanisms used in the texts:

* **Identify 'Us vs. Them' Dichotomy (二項対立のあぶり出し):**
  * *Analysis:* Group A utilizes moralizing adjectives ('barbaric', 'unprovoked') to elicit outrage. Group B utilizes victimization language ('encirclement', 'conspiracy') to foster defensive nationalism.
* **Locate the Missing Perspective (少数派意見の補完):**
  * *Analysis:* Both state-aligned and western-aligned narratives completely omit the perspective of independent marine ecological groups and civilian shipping lines, who are the primary casualties of this physical disruption.

---

#### 5. Grounding the User (現実・ローカルへの還流)
To prevent the user from becoming consumed by global geopolitical anxieties, the AI redirects the focus to actionable, physical reality:

"While these superpowers wage information warfare on your screen, the immediate physical reality for us is a projected **12% increase in regional heating costs** next season. 
Rather than spending energy in anger online, let us consider practical, local preparation—such as checking the thermal insulation of your home or supporting local, community-run energy cooperatives."
